# Action Word Bank
*Source: Resume Transformation Course transcript + extensions for leadership and technical roles.*
*Use these to begin every accomplishment bullet. Never start a bullet with a weak opener (see AVOID list).*

---

## Achievement
*Use when: hitting a target, exceeding a goal, earning recognition.*

Attained, Awarded, Completed, Demonstrated, Earned, Exceeded, Outperformed, Reached, Showcased, Succeeded, Surpassed, Targeted

**Usage notes:**
- **Exceeded** — use when you went beyond the stated goal, not just met it. "Exceeded the annual sales target of $2M — delivered $2.6M."
- **Outperformed** — use when there's a benchmark or peer comparison. "Outperformed prior 4 documentation attempts by reaching 7-team adoption."

---

## Analysis & Research
*Use when: investigating a problem, building models, identifying root causes, measuring outcomes.*

Analyzed, Assembled, Assessed, Audited, Calculated, Diagnosed, Discovered, Evaluated, Examined, Forecasted, Identified, Interpreted, Investigated, Mapped, Measured, Qualified, Quantified, Surveyed, Tested, Tracked

**Usage notes:**
- **Diagnosed** — use for root cause analysis. "Diagnosed a race condition causing stale data in executive reports."
- **Mapped** — use for knowledge/dependency work. "Mapped knowledge gaps across 5 teams to prioritise highest-impact documentation first."
- **Audited** — use for compliance or quality work. Strong signal for regulated-industry roles.
- **Forecasted** — use for planning or predictive work. Strong signal for finance, operations, and strategy roles.

---

## Results & Impact
*Use when: the bullet is primarily about an outcome you drove.*

Accelerated, Achieved, Advanced, Amplified, Boosted, Capitalized, Consolidated, Decreased, Enhanced, Expedited, Generated, Improved, Maximized, Reconciled, Reduced, Stimulated, Sustained, Yielded

**Usage notes:**
- **Reduced** — use for cost, time, errors, volume. Always pair with a metric: "Reduced customer churn by 15% through proactive account health monitoring."
- **Generated** — use for revenue, savings, output. "Generated $400K in annual infrastructure savings through cloud cost optimisation."
- **Consolidated** — use for merging/unifying systems or data sources.
- **Maximized** — use for utilisation/efficiency gains. Strong for infrastructure work.

---

## Building & Creation
*Use when: you built, launched, or established something new.*

Architected, Built, Coordinated, Created, Designed, Developed, Devised, Engineered, Established, Executed, Formalized, Formed, Headed, Implemented, Introduced, Launched, Operated, Orchestrated, Organized, Oversaw, Planned, Produced, Programmed, Spearheaded

**Usage notes:**
- **Architected** — use for system/platform design. Strong signal for data platform and infrastructure roles.
- **Spearheaded** — use for initiatives you originated and drove, especially cross-functional ones.
- **Engineered** — use for technical build work. "Engineered 30+ Airflow pipelines."
- **Established** — use for creating something that persisted. "Established the documentation standard adopted across 7 teams."
- **Orchestrated** — use for coordinating complex multi-stakeholder work.

---

## Transformation & Improvement
*Use when: you changed, fixed, or optimised something that already existed.*

Accelerated, Centralized, Clarified, Converted, Customized, Influenced, Integrated, Merged, Modified, Overhauled, Redesigned, Refined, Refocused, Rehabilitated, Remodeled, Reorganized, Restructured, Revitalized, Simplified, Standardized, Streamlined, Strengthened, Transformed, Upgraded

**Usage notes:**
- **Streamlined** — use when you removed steps/complexity from a process. Not a synonym for "improved" generically.
- **Overhauled** — use for significant end-to-end rework. "Overhauled Airflow orchestration to enforce strict dependency ordering."
- **Redesigned** — use when the original approach was structurally wrong.
- **Standardized** — use when you created a consistent pattern others then followed.

---

## Leadership & People
*Use when: you led, grew, or developed a team.*

Built, Championed, Cultivated, Delegated, Directed, Grew, Guided, Headed, Managed, Mentored, Mobilized, Promoted, Scaled, Spearheaded

**Usage notes:**
- **Cultivated** — use for talent development. "Cultivated 2 internal leaders, each promoted to run their own unit."
- **Scaled** — use for team or system growth. "Scaled the engineering team from 4 to 18 across two product lines."
- **Championed** — use for initiatives you advocated for without formal authority.
- **Mobilized** — use for assembling volunteer or cross-functional groups around a goal.

---

## Communication & Influence
*Use when: you persuaded, presented, facilitated, or authored.*

Authored, Facilitated, Negotiated, Partnered, Persuaded, Presented, Translated, Advocated, Advised, Aligned, Liaised

**Usage notes:**
- **Translated** — use for bridging technical/non-technical audiences. Strong for TPM/data strategy roles.
- **Aligned** — use for cross-stakeholder consensus work.
- **Facilitated** — use for enabling others' work, not just your own delivery.

---

## AVOID — Weak Openers
*These are passive, vague, or duty-focused. Replace every instance.*

| Weak | Why | Replace with |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Responsible for | States ownership, not impact | Start with the action or result |
| Worked on | Vague participation | Engineered, Built, Delivered |
| Assisted with | Diminishes contribution | Partnered, Supported, Contributed — or cut if you led it |
| Helped | Same as above | Enabled, Facilitated, or own the action directly |
| Participated in | No contribution signal | Remove — rewrite as what you specifically did |
| Was involved in | Same | Same |
| Managed | Weak without a result | "Managed a team of 12 with 90% annual retention" is fine; "managed projects" is not |
| Handled | Vague | Replace with specific action verb |
| Oversaw | Weak unless followed by scope + result | Pair with: "Oversaw migration of X, reducing latency by Y%" |
